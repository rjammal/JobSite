My Story

Ever since my first computer science class in college, I've been interested in programming, but I wasn't sure at first that it was what I wanted to do full time. I had to try a few different fields first (linguistics, cognitive science, education, finance) to see what my options were.

The real turning point in my life was when I started working at Advent Software. It was my gateway back in to the tech industry. I started in technical support, and I loved it. Each day was a new problem to solve, a new part of the software to learn, a new deliverable that a client needed for their business. I was responsible for keeping the software running in high uptime enterprise environments, solving and proving out complex calculations, finding and correcting bad data, and much more. I loved the challenge and the variety, and I rose in the ranks from junior support representative, to senior, to analyst within the first two years.

I rose quickly, but I also found myself levelling out. My day to day became more and more routine and less and less challenging. Needing something more engaging, I signed up for an introductory Python course on Coursera. It was during that course that I realized that programming was more than just a hobby; this was what I wanted to be doing all day every day.

Even after the course concluded, I continued to practice and work on coding with any free time I had. I started attending meetups and became a leader in the Women Who Code organization, hosting a weekly Python meetup for about a year. I also negotiated with my employer to work part time with our development staff. I worked with C# as a full stack engineer, creating software vital to our QA. I added several features to our internal testing software that helped automate and streamline the testing process.

I loved my work at Advent, but I found myself torn by the part time nature of the work. I wanted to be coding full time, but there just wasn't headcount to move me the rest of the way. I was sad to leave, but in the end, I had to leave Advent so I could pursue programming as a full time profession. That was when I started at AppAcademy.

AppAcademy allowed me to pour myself into programming full time. I finally got to program all day without having to stop. That was a big step up, but I also found myself missing the direction and purpose of being on a team with a common goal and concrete deliverables. Right now, I'm eager to jump into a new team and start work on a new project.
